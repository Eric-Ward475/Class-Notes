- Written by Guy de Maupassant

**Summary**
- Tells the Story of Mathilde Loisel and her husband
	- She is a shallow, selfish and self-centered woman, constantly wanting more from her middle-class life
- Her husband was invited to a fancy dance
- She did not want to look poor and lowly
	- She purchased a 400 franc (French currency) dress to wear, with the money that her husband had been saving to purchase a hunting gun
		- This shows her selfishness and greed, along with her husband's selflessness and kindness
	- She also borrowed a necklace from her wealthy friend
- After the dance, she noticed that the necklace was missing
	- Her husband went out searching for it, but could not find it
	- She sat at home doing nothing to find the necklace
- Mathilde told her friend that she was getting the necklace fixed at a repair shop, because she had broken the clasp
	- During this time, Mathilde and her husband looked for a perfect replacement of the necklace
		- They went from jeweler to jeweler, looking for a replacement, with no luck
		- Eventually, they found one, costing 3600 francs
	- Mathilde's husband purchased the necklace, taking loans, and borrowing money any way he could
- It took the family 10 years to pay for the necklace
	- Mathilde had to take all the work on in the household
	- They lived in poverty for those years
- After everything had been paid back, Mathilde told her old friend the truth of where the necklace came from
	- Her friend revealed that her necklace was an artificial one, not real
