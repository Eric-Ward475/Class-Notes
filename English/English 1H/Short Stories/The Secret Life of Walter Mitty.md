**Overview**
- Written by James Thurber
- Tells of Walter Mitty, an adult living a boring life, who finds meaning and change in his imagination

**Summary**
 - Walter is having a fantasy when the story opens
	 - He is the commander of a large navy ship
	 - The ship is sailing the the worst storm in 20 years
	 - He pictures him as brave and fearless
	 - The crew on board follow all of his commands without a question
- He is awakened from his fantasy by his wife telling him to slow down
	- They are driving to a town to run some errands
- Walter drops his wife off for a haircut
- His wife reminds him to buy a new pair of overshoes, which he does not want to do
	- She responds to him not wanting them by telling him that he does need them, because he is no longer a young and strong man
- She also points out that he is not wearing his gloves, which he also did not want to wear
- Mitty puts on the gloves, but as soon as his wife is out of sight, takes them off
- As Walter is driving past a hospital, he begins another daydream
	- He is a world famous doctor
	- He arrived at an operating room, where the doctors assigned to the patient were unable to perform the operation
	- A life support machine failed, and Walter temporarily fixed it with a fountain pen
	- The doctors begged him to take over the operation, and he did
- He falls out of his daydream when he entered a parking lot and the attendant tells him that he is entering the lot through the exit only lane
	- The attendant embarrasses him, by parking Walter's car for him
- After purchasing the overshoes that his wife told him to buy, he was struggling to remember what else his wife had told him to pick up from the grocery store
- A newsboy was shouting news about an ongoing trial, causing Mitty to slip into another daydream
	- This time, Walter is the defendant at a murder trial
	- Rather than denying that he is skilled with firearms, he chose to brag about his expertise, which causes the court room to descent into chaos
- Walter exited the daydream by randomly announcing 'puppy biscuit'
- A woman is walking past when he does this, and she laughed at him
- He had earlier agreed to meet up with Mrs. Mitty
	- While he was waiting for her, he read a magazine about the German air force
	- Mitty entered another daydream
		- He was a WWI pilot, high ranking
		- Mitty volunteered to fly in a dangerous solo bombing mission
		- He fearlessly takes the mission, knowing that it is almost certain that he will die
- His wife hits him, bringing him back to the real world
- She nags him for being difficult to find, and ignored Walter when he asked if she ever considers that he may me thinking
- Walter entered into a final fantasy, this time of him fearlessly standing before a firing squad, accepting death
